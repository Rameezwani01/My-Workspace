THE BEAUTIFUL KASHMIR

. . .If there's Paradise on Earth

It's This, It's This, It's This. . .

"A Poem about Kashmir"

In Kashmir's valleys, mountains tall and grand,

Where nature's artistry paints the land.

A tapestry of beauty, serene and pure,

In every corner, a landscape to allure.


Majestic Himalayas, their peaks so high,

Kiss the azure sky, where eagles fly.

Snow-capped crowns glisten in the sun's embrace,

A tranquil haven, a sacred space.


Dal Lake's reflection, a mirror so still,

Shikaras glide, a dream to fulfill.

Lotus blooms dance in waters so clear,

As whispers of legends linger near.


Chinar trees, with leaves of crimson hue,

In autumn's embrace, they bid adieu.

Yet in their branches, stories they hold,

Of love and loss, of stories untold.


The Mughal gardens, a timeless delight,

With terraced beauty, a splendid sight.

Where history's echoes softly hum,

In every corner, tales become.


Kashmir, a land of poets and dreams,

Where saffron fields paint golden seams.

In its strife, a yearning for peace,

Where love and hope shall never cease.


Though conflict's shadow may cloud the day,

Kashmir's spirit will find its way.

A land of beauty, resilient and strong,

In verses and songs, its heartbeats throng.


In Kashmir's embrace, let us aspire,

To find harmony, to quench the fire.

For in its essence, it's not defined,

By borders or strife, but the love it binds.


Kashmir, oh Kashmir, in you, we find,

A timeless beauty, a hopeful mind.

May your story be one of peace, we pray,

In your valleys and mountains, come what may.
